The Porch

◯ ◯ ◯ ◯

This is the threshold space—neither fully inside nor out, where we sit with what is and wonder about what might be.

What Actually Exists

Right now, this is a simple gallery site. Forty-three paintings spanning seven years. Some linocuts. A CV. An artist statement. Basic navigation. Clean presentation. It loads fast, works on phones, shows the work clearly.

This is sufficient. This is honest. This serves the paintings.

What Might Grow

But there's this restlessness. This sense that the digital space could hold more—not flashy things, not clever things, but things that serve the deeper work. Things that connect rather than distract. Things that invite contemplation rather than demand attention.

So we sit here on this porch and let the mind wander...

The Wandering Mind

What if the same contemplative attention that goes into painting could shape how we encounter digital space?

Some Gentle Possibilities

A Simple Map

Maybe just a small map showing where these paintings were made. Moscow, The Hague, that residency in Japan. Not spinning globes or fancy WebGL—just a quiet way to see how place shapes practice.

Process Notes

Sometimes there are sketches, failed attempts, photos of the studio at 3am. The messiness behind the finished work. These could live somewhere, for people who are curious about the making.

Slow Letters

An occasional email to people who want to follow the work. Not newsletter-marketing, just honest updates about what's happening in the studio. What's being painted, what's being read, what's being learned.

Guest Contemplations

Other artists, writers, or thinkers responding to the work. Not reviews or critiques, but genuine encounters. What do they see? What does it evoke? How does it connect to their own practice?

Material Studies

The technical side: how these colors are mixed, which surfaces work best, what happens to paintings over time. For people who want to understand the craft as well as the vision.

Quiet Conversations

A simple forum or space for people to share what they see in the work. Not social media chaos, but thoughtful exchanges. What memories do these colors evoke? What do they notice that others might miss?

Small Prints

Some of these paintings could live in homes as high-quality prints. Not mass production, but careful reproductions for people who want to sit with the work daily. Perhaps some original smaller pieces too.

Open Studios

Occasionally opening the studio to small groups. Not performances or presentations, just the chance to see work in progress, to understand the scale and texture that photos can't capture.

Contemplative Tech

What if digital tools could slow us down instead of speed us up? Simple apps that help people look more carefully, breathe more deeply, notice more fully. Technology in service of presence, not distraction.

The Honest Question

Which of these might actually serve the work? Which might connect with people in meaningful ways? Which might grow slowly and sustainably, without demanding more attention than they deserve?

Not everything needs to be built. Not every possibility needs to be pursued. The porch is a place for sitting with options, not for rushing toward answers.

What's True Right Now

The paintings exist. People look at them. Some are moved by them. Some buy them. Some commission new ones. The basic ecology of an art practice functions.

These digital extensions—if they happen—should strengthen that ecology, not complicate it. They should serve the deeper work, not distract from it.

◯ ◯ ◯ ◯

The Deeper Question

How do we use digital tools to become more present, more connected, more awake—rather than more distracted, more scattered, more numb?

This is the invitation that matters: to approach technology with the same contemplative attention we bring to painting. To build only what serves. To connect only in ways that honor the depth of real encounter.

The Porch Remains

This threshold space will stay. A place to return to when the excitement of new possibilities needs to be balanced with the wisdom of patient consideration.

Some of these ideas might grow into reality. Others might remain as daydreams. Both outcomes are fine. The porch is not a planning committee—it's a place for honest dreaming.

◯ ◯ ◯ ◯

What needs to happen will happen. What needs to wait will wait. The practice continues.
